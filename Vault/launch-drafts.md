## 2. Press Release

**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**
**March 18, 2026**

**Hromada Launches Platform Connecting US Donors with Ukrainian Municipalities for Renewable Energy Infrastructure**

**Washington, DC** — Hromada, a new online platform, launched today to connect American donors with Ukrainian municipalities seeking philanthropic funding for decentralized renewable energy infrastructure.

The platform features projects submitted directly by Ukrainian communities — solar PV arrays, heat pumps, battery storage systems, and thermo-modernization for hospitals, schools, water utilities, and other public facilities. Each project is verified by NGO partners on the ground before publication.

Hromada's first partner is NGO Ecoaction, Ukraine's largest environmental organization. Ecoaction's Energy Department, headed by Kostiantyn Krynytskyi, has delivered renewable energy projects across Ukraine including a ground-source heat pump and hybrid solar system for the Horenka Primary Healthcare Center near Bucha — a project that cut heating costs by 80% and electricity costs by 41%.

"Ukrainian communities are identifying what they need to rebuild and requesting support on their own terms," said Thomas Protzman, founder of Hromada. "The platform carries those requests to donors who want to fund specific, verified infrastructure projects."

All municipal procurement is conducted through Prozorro, Ukraine's mandatory public e-procurement platform, where every transaction is publicly listed and auditable. NGO partners provide on-site documentation during construction and monitoring data once systems are operational.

Hromada is currently live in directory mode, with additional features planned for the coming weeks. Donors can browse projects, review documentation, and schedule calls with project teams at hromadaproject.org.

**About Hromada**

Hromada — "community" in Ukrainian — connects municipalities with donors to rebuild critical civilian infrastructure, focusing on renewable energy and resilience. The platform exclusively supports civilian projects. Hromada does not fund, facilitate, or support any military equipment, dual-use technology, or defense-related projects.
