## Three Hardest Questions

### 1. "How is this not putting POCACITO's name on a war?"
Hromada funds civilian municipal infrastructure -- heating systems, water filtration, solar panels, school repairs. Zero military involvement. The implementing partners are environmental NGOs: Ecoaction, Ecoclub Rivne, Greenpeace Ukraine. The municipalities themselves identify what they need, our partners verify the projects and provide documentation -- cost estimates, engineering assessments, site photos -- and Hromada is the platform that connects them with American donors. The money flows from donors into POCACITO's account, then directly to the requesting municipality. No intermediary handles the funds. POCACITO's name is on municipal energy resilience and sustainability -- which is already the mission.

### 2. "How do we verify funds reach their intended purpose in an active conflict zone?"
The money goes directly from POCACITO to the municipality's bank account -- our NGO partners never touch it. That's by design. The partners' role is project vetting: they work with the municipalities, provide cost estimates, engineering assessments, site photos, and procurement documentation. Ukraine has a public procurement system (Prozorro) that makes government spending transparent and auditable. After completion, partners provide photographic evidence and completion reports. We also maintain an OFAC compliance policy and screen all recipients. Every project requires documentation before it's even listed on the platform -- we prioritize projects with stronger third-party verification. And POCACITO retains oversight through the FSA -- there's an audit flow-through provision.

### 3. "How does funding Ukrainian municipalities fit POCACITO's mission?"
POCACITO's mission is transatlantic sustainability. This is transatlantic sustainability. Ukrainian municipalities are replacing Soviet-era fossil fuel infrastructure with modern, efficient systems -- solar, heat pumps, insulated buildings, energy-independent heating. Every project is a climate project. The transatlantic piece is literal: American donors funding European municipal energy resilience. And the partners delivering this -- Ecoaction, Ecoclub Rivne, Greenpeace Ukraine -- are environmental organizations. This isn't mission creep. It's POCACITO's mission applied to the place where it's most needed and most impactful right now. The 0% fee structure reflects that -- this is mission-aligned, not a revenue play.
