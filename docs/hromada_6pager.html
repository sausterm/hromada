The Strategic Problem

Russia systematically targets Ukraine's centralized energy infrastructure to break civilian morale. Rolling blackouts and freezing winters are designed to wear down society's will to defend itself. This is not collateral damage — it is a deliberate theory of victory.

Why Traditional Aid Fails

Centralized channels miss local needs

Most aid flows through national mechanisms. Municipalities know what they need but lack access to international donors.

Prohibitive fees

Platforms like GlobalGiving charge 8–15% per donation. On $50K, that's $4K–7.5K that never reaches a community.

No individual accountability

Donors have no visibility into which community their money supports or whether it was spent as intended.

The Resilience Argument

Every decentralized renewable installation makes Russia's strategy harder to execute. When a school has its own solar array, it cannot be shut down by striking a central power plant.

This transforms the narrative from humanitarian aid to strategic investment in civilian resilience.

Hromada funds civilian infrastructure only. Hospitals, schools, water utilities, and community energy systems.

How the Platform Works

Every project on Hromada passes through a nine-step process designed for maximum accountability. Checkpoints highlighted in blue.

1
Community Request
A Ukrainian municipality identifies a renewable energy need. Their on-the-ground NGO partner scopes the project, produces a cost estimate, and submits it to Hromada.
2
Pre-Screening & Due Diligence
Sanctions screening of all associated individuals, transparency score checks, government control verification.
Checkpoint: OFAC sanctions screening, government control verification
3
Published on Platform
Verified project appears with full details: municipality, oblast, project type, cost breakdown, NGO partner, co-financing status.
4
Donor Discovery & Consultation
Donor browses the platform, selects a project, and books a brief consultation with the Hromada team.
5
Donation via Wire Transfer or DAF
Funds sent via wire or Donor Advised Fund to the fiscal sponsor's US bank account. Only full-project funding accepted.
Checkpoint: Tax-deductible receipt issued by 501(c)(3) fiscal sponsor
6
Second-Round Sanctions Check
Second OFAC screening before any disbursement.
Checkpoint: Dual sanctions screening (pre-listing + pre-disbursement)
7
Legal Framework Execution
NGO partners sign MoUs; municipalities sign contracts committing funds to intended purpose. All procurement via Prozorro.
Checkpoint: MoU framework, municipal contracts, Prozorro transparency
8
Direct Bank-to-Bank Disbursement
SWIFT wire from the fiscal sponsor's US bank account directly to the Ukrainian municipality. No intermediaries.
9
Donor Dashboard & Progress Tracking
Secure dashboard with tax receipt, status timeline, project updates, photos, receipts, and Prozorro links.
Checkpoint: Full visibility — request through funding through completion

hromada From Ukrainian communities to American donors Transparency & Accountability

Total Transparency. From Request to Completion.

Hromada's architecture is built around a single principle: donors should be able to trace every dollar from their bank account to the municipality that uses it.

Sanctions Compliance

Dual-layer OFAC screening: once before a project is listed, and again before funds are disbursed. All individuals associated with the requesting municipality are screened.

Procurement

All procurement must go through Prozorro, Ukraine's public procurement platform. Contractor selection is fully transparent, competitive, and verifiable by donors.

MoU Framework

NGO partners sign Memoranda of Understanding defining obligations: project verification, monitoring, monthly progress reports with photos, and completion reports. 14-day response SLA.

Municipal Contracts

NGO partners ensure municipalities sign contracts committing to use funds for the intended project — with legal recourse. Binding chain of accountability from donor to municipality.

Donor Dashboard

Every donor receives a secure dashboard with: tax-deductible receipt, donation timeline, project progress updates from the NGO partner including photos, receipts, and Prozorro procurement links. Full visibility from request through funding through completion.

How Funds Are Protected

Every disbursement is governed by an MoU between Hromada and the NGO partner. Partners maintain full expenditure records, report monthly, and procure exclusively through Prozorro. Funds are restricted to the stated civilian project scope.
