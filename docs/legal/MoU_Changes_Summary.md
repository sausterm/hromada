# MoU Updates — Summary for Partners

**Date:** March 6, 2026
**From:** Tom Protzman

---

## What changed since the version you reviewed

We ran the MoU through a comprehensive legal review covering US nonprofit law, OFAC/sanctions compliance, Ukrainian civil law, donor disclosure requirements, and international development due diligence. The core structure and obligations are the same. Here's what was added or refined:

### New sections

- **Section 4.0 — Partner Qualifications.** Before signing, each partner provides basic organizational documents: registration, key personnel list, financial report, org profile, and funding source disclosure. Hromada screens partner personnel against OFAC lists before execution. This is standard due diligence that protects both sides.

- **Section 4.6 — Audit and Inspection.** Hromada reserves the right to audit partner records and project sites at its own expense. This is a standard provision for any US nonprofit working with international partners.

### Revised sections

- **Section 2 — Purpose.** Added a sentence clarifying the MoU is non-binding and reflects mutual good-faith intentions.

- **Section 3(c) — Implementation Support.** Clarified that the partner acts as an independent advisor and monitor, not a sub-grantee or agent of Hromada. All procurement decisions and payments remain the municipality's responsibility.

- **Section 4.2(b) — Fund misuse notification.** "Promptly" replaced with "within five (5) business days" for clarity.

- **Section 4.3(d) — Response time.** Reduced from 14 calendar days to 7 calendar days.

- **Section 4.4(a)-(c) — Sanctions language.** "Not knowingly" replaced with "exercise reasonable due diligence." This aligns with OFAC's actual enforcement standard.

- **Section 4.4(b) — Territory definition.** Updated from a specific list (Crimea, DPR, LPR) to "any territory not under effective control of the Government of Ukraine." Added clarification that relocated communities are evaluated based on current operating location, not pre-displacement registration.

- **Section 4.4(e) — Screening timing.** Split into three tiers: known municipal officials provided before project publication; contractors/vendors provided within 5 business days of selection (since they aren't known before procurement); subsequent personnel changes within 5 business days. No funds disbursed until screening is complete.

- **Section 4.4(g) — Anti-terrorism.** New clause: partner will not provide material support to designated terrorist organizations. Standard US compliance language.

- **Section 4.9 — Content license.** Added a right to request removal of specific content after termination for privacy or legal reasons.

- **Section 4.10 — Fund disbursement.** Bolded the key sentence: the partner never receives, holds, or disburses Hromada funds. No change in substance — just emphasis.

- **Section 6 — Retitled** from "No Financial Obligation" to "Financial Terms and Co-Financing Commitments" (the old title was misleading given the co-financing provisions). Added co-financing disclosure consent and a "Failed Projects" paragraph addressing what happens if a project fails for reasons other than co-financing withdrawal.

- **Section 7 — Language.** MoU is now executed in both English and Ukrainian, with English controlling in case of conflict. Operational correspondence may be in either language.

- **Section 11(c) — Liability cap.** Added: aggregate liability of either party capped at total Hromada funds disbursed for projects involving the partner.

- **Section 12(c) — Cure period.** 15-day cure period for non-material breaches before termination. Sanctions, corruption, and post-publication co-financing withdrawal remain grounds for immediate termination.

- **Section 12(d) — Project suspension.** Hromada can suspend an individual project without terminating the entire MoU. 30-day resolution target.

- **Section 13 — Dispute resolution.** Replaced mediation-only with binding arbitration through the Stockholm Chamber of Commerce (SCC). Arbitral awards are enforceable in Ukrainian courts under the New York Convention. Virginia governing law retained.

- **Section 16 — Signatures.** Thomas Protzman's title filled in as "Project Director." POCACITO board authorization referenced in Section 1, with Exhibit A placeholder for the resolution.

### What didn't change

The partner's core role (identify, verify, monitor, report) is unchanged. The fund-flow model is unchanged — funds go directly to municipal bank accounts, partners never touch donor money. Co-financing terms are unchanged. Reporting frequency is unchanged. The MoU remains a one-year term with 30-day termination notice.
