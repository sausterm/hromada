## 1. Google.org Impact Challenge: AI for Government Innovation

### URGENT — DEADLINE: APRIL 3, 2026

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Funder** | Google.org |
| **Grant Size** | $1M–$3M USD + up to $200K in Google Cloud credits |
| **Duration** | Up to 36 months |
| **Status** | **OPEN NOW — Deadline April 3, 2026 at 11:59 PM PT** |
| **Total Fund** | $30M global |
| **Fiscal Sponsorship** | Yes — Impact Challenges accept nonprofits, social enterprises, and even for-profits with social impact purpose |

### Program Overview
This is the single most relevant currently-open opportunity for Hromada. Google.org is awarding $1M–$3M grants to organizations using AI to improve government services and community resilience. Two focus areas are directly relevant:

- **Resilience:** Empowering communities to plan, respond, and recover from crises using real-time data and AI-powered forecasting
- **Economy:** Expanding opportunity and affordability by improving public infrastructure, optimizing resource allocation

### Eligibility
- Nonprofits, academic institutions, social enterprises, and for-profits with social impact missions
- Must demonstrate partnership with a government entity
- For-profit applicants must be willing to open-source IP
- Maximum 2 applications per organization
- Applications must be in English
- In-person attendance required at Kickoff (September 2026) and Demo Day (December 2026)

### What Google Looks For
1. **Impact** — Address a critical public service challenge; drive measurable outcomes; strong government partnerships
2. **Innovative Use of Technology** — Leverage generative or agentic AI beyond incremental change
3. **Feasibility** — Realistic execution plan, capable team, documented government buy-in
4. **Scalability & Sustainability** — Potential for replication across governments/regions; plan beyond grant period

### Application Strategy for Hromada

**Framing:**
Position Hromada as an AI-powered reconstruction coordination platform that:
- Uses AI for needs assessment prioritization across Ukrainian municipalities
- Employs intelligent donor-project matching based on donor interests, project urgency, and funding gaps
- Provides AI-driven progress monitoring and outcome prediction
- Generates transparency and accountability reports automatically

**Required Government Partnership:**
Secure formal documentation from Ukrainian municipal authorities and/or the Ministry of Digital Transformation. Ukraine has a strategic relationship with Google on AI in government and aims to be top 3 in AI adoption by 2030 under its Digital Agenda 2030.

**Key Narrative Points:**
- $588B reconstruction need with a $45B shortfall in 2026 alone
- USAID closure created a coordination gap — Hromada fills it with AI-powered transparency
- Platform already has interactive map, project filtering, and contact facilitation
- AI layer adds intelligent matching, prioritization, and monitoring
- Scalable beyond Ukraine to any post-conflict or disaster recovery context
- Aligns with Ukraine's WINWIN 2030 Innovation Strategy and Digital Agenda 2030

**Metrics to Include:**
- Number of municipalities served
- Number of infrastructure projects tracked
- Donor-to-project match rate and speed
- Reduction in time from need identification to funding
- AI accuracy in needs prioritization

**Application Portal:** https://googlenewaccount2.submittable.com/submit/b77643df-9f35-4379-8fa1-fe7371318081/google-org-impact-challenge-ai-for-government-innovation

## 2. Nefco Green Recovery Programme for Ukraine

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Funder** | Nordic Environment Finance Corporation (Sweden, Denmark, Finland, Norway, EU) |
| **Total Mobilized** | EUR 360M+ |
| **Status** | Active — rolling applications, no fixed deadline |
| **Application Type** | Relationship-driven; municipalities submit proposals directly to Nefco |
| **Fiscal Sponsorship** | N/A — municipalities are the primary applicants |

### Program Overview
The Nefco Green Recovery Programme funds repair and reconstruction of municipal infrastructure in Ukraine in an environmentally sound way. Over 60 projects ongoing, 30+ completed since full-scale invasion. Phase 2 (2025+) uses blended finance (loans + grants).

### What It Funds
- Energy efficiency in public buildings (schools, hospitals, daycare centres)
- District heating repair, upgrade, modernization
- Water supply and wastewater management
- Municipal solid waste management
- Renewable energy installations
- Housing for internally displaced persons (IDPs)
- Capacity building for Local Green Recovery Plans

### How It Works
1. Municipality submits project proposal to Nefco
2. Nefco assesses alignment and fund availability
3. Nefco procures third-party feasibility study
4. Grant/loan agreement signed
5. Contractors selected through procurement
6. **Funds go directly from Nefco to contractors — never through the municipality**
7. Projects monitored for environmental/sustainability compliance

### Application Strategy for Hromada

Hromada cannot apply directly as a grant recipient (only municipalities can). Instead, pursue **partnership pathways:**

1. **Technology Platform Partner:** Position Hromada as the digital infrastructure for municipalities to discover, document, and publicize their recovery needs, making them more attractive to Nefco and other donors
2. **Complement Local Green Recovery Plans:** Nefco requires municipalities to develop Local Green Recovery Plans. Hromada could provide the digital framework for these plans
3. **Technical Assistance Consultant:** Bid on future RFPs for technical assistance (project selection, needs assessment, digital project management)
4. **Donor Visibility Layer:** Offer to serve as the public-facing discovery platform for Nefco-funded projects, increasing transparency and attracting co-funding
