## Executive Summary

Hromada is a technology platform connecting donors with Ukrainian municipalities that need support for critical infrastructure — hospitals, schools, water utilities, and energy systems. The platform features an interactive map, project filtering, and a contact facilitation system.

The grant landscape for Ukraine reconstruction is substantial (~$588 billion estimated need over 10 years), but has shifted significantly since the closure of USAID in mid-2025. European institutions have become the dominant funding channel, and there is growing emphasis on renewable energy, decentralized power, and civic technology platforms that improve aid transparency.

This research identifies **15+ grant opportunities** across three tiers of fit, ranging from direct infrastructure funding to technology nonprofit grants.

---

## Funding Landscape Context

### The Shift from US to European Funding
- **USAID closed on July 1, 2025**, with 83% of programs cut. ~$9 billion in humanitarian funds clawed back.
- The USAID Energy Security Project (ESP) for Ukraine — the largest non-humanitarian US program — was terminated.
- **Europe has stepped up** but has not fully offset the US drawdown. The EU's Ukraine Facility provides up to **EUR 50 billion** (2024–2027) in grants and loans.
- Ukraine faces a **$45 billion shortfall** for social/civilian spending in 2026.

### Scale of Need
- Total reconstruction cost: **$588 billion** over 10 years (World Bank, Feb 2026).
- Ukraine has **$5.8 billion** available for priority projects in 2026, with a **$9.5 billion deficit**.
- Key sectors: energy (40% of EU investment), transport (9%), social housing (6%), water (5%).

### Opportunity for Hromada
The collapse of USAID funding creates a **transparency and coordination gap** — exactly the problem Hromada solves. European funders increasingly demand accountability mechanisms, and a technology platform that connects donors directly to verified municipal projects is highly aligned with this need.

## Tier 1 — High-Fit Grant Opportunities

These grants are the strongest match for Hromada's mission and current stage.

### 1. Nefco Green Recovery Programme for Ukraine
- **Funder:** Nordic Environment Finance Corporation (Denmark, Sweden, Finland, EU)
- **Total mobilized:** EUR 360 million+
- **What it funds:** Repair and reconstruction of municipal infrastructure in an environmentally sound way; energy efficiency; renewable energy; district heating; water/wastewater
- **How it works:** Municipalities submit project proposals to Nefco. Nefco procures feasibility studies, then funds contractors directly (no funds pass through municipalities).
- **Second Phase (2025–2026):** Moving to blended finance (loans + grants) for public buildings, energy efficiency, and renewables.
- **Hromada angle:** Hromada could serve as a discovery/coordination layer for municipalities seeking Nefco funding, or partner with Nefco to surface verified projects. Could also apply as a technology partner for project pipeline management.
- **Status:** Ongoing / rolling applications
- **URL:** https://www.nefco.int/financing/municipalities-in-eastern-europe/green-recovery-ukraine/

### 2. Elrha Humanitarian Innovation Fund (HIF)
- **Funder:** Elrha (UK-based, multi-donor)
- **What it funds:** Innovation in humanitarian response — technology platforms, new approaches to aid delivery, locally-led solutions
- **Grant size:** Varies by call; typically GBP 50K–250K
- **Focus areas (2026):** AI in humanitarian settings, locally-led innovation, tech platforms for crisis response, equity and inclusion
- **Hromada angle:** Hromada is a textbook HIF candidate — a technology platform improving humanitarian coordination and donor-to-municipality transparency in a conflict zone.
- **How to apply:** Through the Common Grant Application platform; watch for open calls.
- **Strategy:** 2023–2040 strategy emphasizes locally-led innovation and private sector partnerships.
- **URL:** https://www.elrha.org/funding

### 3. Google.org — Democratic Resilience & Civic Technology (Europe)
- **Funder:** Google.org
- **Grant:** EUR 15 million open call for European nonprofits and civic entities
- **What it funds:** Initiatives promoting democratic resilience, civic technology, AI-powered social solutions
- **Hromada angle:** Hromada directly strengthens civic resilience by connecting communities with reconstruction resources. The platform's transparency features align with Google.org's democratic resilience focus.
- **Total Google.org giving:** ~$6 billion over two decades; $120 million AI Opportunity Fund
- **URL:** https://www.google.org/our-work/

### 4. EU Ukraine Investment Framework (UIF) — Technology & Innovation Track
- **Funder:** European Commission / EIB
- **Total committed:** EUR 9.4 billion (incl. EUR 1.6 billion in grants)
- **What it funds:** Green/renewable energy, digitalization, manufacturing, agri-business; private sector unlocking via Foreign Direct Investment
- **Hromada angle:** The UIF explicitly funds "digitalization" and platforms that mobilize investment. Hromada's donor-matching platform fits within the digital infrastructure category.
- **Implementation:** 2024–2027 via EIB and partner institutions
- **URL:** https://enlargement.ec.europa.eu/countries/ukraine/ukraine-investment-framework_en

## Key Considerations & Risks

### Legal & Organizational Requirements
- **501(c)(3) status:** Many US-based tech grants (Tech Impact, Cisco, Okta) require recognized nonprofit status. If Hromada does not yet have this, it should be prioritized.
- **EU entity requirements:** Some European grants require an EU-based legal entity or partnership with one.
- **Partnership strategy:** For larger institutional grants (EIB, World Bank, Nefco), Hromada may need to partner with an established implementing organization rather than apply directly.

### USAID / US Government Funding
- USAID is closed. US government funding for Ukraine civilian aid is at its lowest point since the full-scale invasion.
- The 2026 NDAA includes only $400 million for the Ukraine Security Assistance Initiative (military only).
- **Recommendation:** Focus grant efforts on European and multilateral sources, not US government channels.

### Political Risk
- US-brokered peace talks may cause some donors to prematurely reduce funding.
- European political shifts could affect EU Ukraine Facility disbursements.
- **Mitigation:** Diversify across multiple funding sources and geographies.

### Security Considerations
- Hromada already uses city/town-level coordinates (not exact addresses) to avoid creating a targetable infrastructure database. This is a key selling point for security-conscious funders.
